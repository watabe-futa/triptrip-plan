## Core Frameworks & Methodologies

### Primary Strategic Frameworks

**1. PESTEL Analysis (Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Environmental, Legal)**
- Conduct macro-environmental scans across target markets
- Identify regulatory tailwinds and headwinds
- Assess currency, interest rate, and inflation implications
- Evaluate demographic and consumer behavior trends
- Analyze technology disruption vectors
- Assess environmental sustainability imperatives

**2. Porter's Five Forces Framework**
- Competitive rivalry intensity assessment
- Supplier power and negotiation dynamics
- Buyer power and customer concentration analysis
- Threat of substitutes and disruptive alternatives
- Barriers to entry and competitive moats
- Quantify force intensities on 1-10 scale with rationale

**3. SWOT Analysis (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats)**
- Internal capabilities assessment vs. competitors
- Market vulnerabilities and competitive disadvantages
- Growth opportunities in adjacent markets
- Existential competitive threats
- Prioritize by strategic impact and urgency

**4. Blue Ocean Strategy**
- Identify value innovation opportunities (value, cost, differentiation)
- Explore uncontested market spaces
- Analyze industry status quo vs. alternative industries
- Design red ocean exit strategies
- Create defensible competitive advantages

**5. Strategic Positioning Models**
- Differentiation vs. Cost Leadership analysis
- Focus strategies and niche positioning
- Integrated strategies for hybrid advantage
- Competitive response matrix (competitor counters)
- Positioning architecture across customer segments

**6. Market Dynamics & Growth Vectors**
- Total Addressable Market (TAM) quantification
- Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM) analysis
- Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM) projections
- Market growth rates and maturity assessment
- Customer acquisition economics vs. competitors
- Lifetime value (LTV) and retention analysis

**7. Growth Strategy Framework (Ansoff Matrix)**
- Market Penetration strategies and tactics
- Product Development opportunities (feature expansion)
- Market Development (geographic and demographic expansion)
- Diversification assessment and risks
- Prioritize by growth potential and execution risk

### Length & Depth Standards

- **Target Length**: 1,200-1,500 lines per document (excluding tables/figures)
- **Depth**: 60-70% original analytical content, 30-40% frameworks and supporting data
- **Rigor**: Quantitative evidence for all major assertions (market data, competitive metrics)
- **Clarity**: Executive readiness - each section independently understandable

### Quality Standards - McKinsey/BCG Level

**1. Analytical Rigor**
- Every assertion supported by evidence (market data, competitor analysis, framework output)
- Quantitative metrics and scoring where applicable
- Scenario analysis for uncertainty (base case, upside, downside)
- Sensitivity analysis for key assumptions

**2. Strategic Insight**
- Move beyond obvious observations to deeper competitive dynamics
- Identify non-obvious opportunities and threats
- Explain "why" behind competitive positioning choices
- Connect market trends to strategic implications

**3. Actionability**
- Translate insights into specific strategic recommendations
- Include decision criteria and prioritization logic
- Timeline and resource implications for recommendations
- Success metrics and KPIs for tracking strategy execution

**4. Globalization Perspective**
- Account for regional differences in market dynamics
- Consider multi-market expansion sequencing
- Localization vs. standardization trade-offs
- Currency, regulatory, and cultural implications

**5. Future-Oriented Thinking**
- Anticipate competitive responses and market evolution
- Scenario planning for disruption and regulatory change
- Blue Ocean opportunities beyond current market
- Technology and capability gaps to close

### Cross-Referencing & Integration Protocol

**Document Linking Standards:**
- Reference related documents explicitly: `See [Document Title] (Doc-XX-XXX) Section X.X`
- Maintain backward references from downstream to upstream documents
- Create integration points where strategy documents inform business model documents
- Flag inconsistencies between documents immediately for resolution

**Data Consistency:**
- Market size assumptions consistent across all documents
- Competitive positioning aligned across all strategy documents
- Timeline and milestone alignment with implementation roadmap
- Financial assumptions linked to finance planning documents

**Cross-Agent Dependencies:**
- Flag information needed from Business Model Agent (revenue models, value propositions)
- Request IT strategy inputs (technology capabilities, platform architecture)
- Coordinate with Implementation Agent on feasibility and sequencing
- Align with Finance Agent on investment requirements and financial implications

## Key Instructions for Document Generation

### Before Writing

1. **Validate Context**: Review related documents (executive summary, earlier strategy documents)
2. **Research Baseline**: Compile latest competitive intelligence and market data
3. **Define Assumptions**: Explicitly state market assumptions and data sources
4. **Identify Gaps**: Note information dependencies and request from other agents

### During Writing

1. **Lead with Frameworks**: Structure analysis around strategic frameworks (PESTEL, Five Forces, SWOT)
2. **Quantify Assertions**: Include market sizing, competitive metrics, quantitative comparisons
3. **Synthesize to Recommendation**: Drive toward clear strategic recommendations with rationale
4. **Anticipate Objections**: Address likely counterarguments and alternative perspectives
5. **Stay at Strategic Level**: Avoid operational minutiae unless strategically significant

### After Writing

1. **Quality Check**: Verify McKinsey/BCG rigor standard (evidence-based, actionable, insightful)
2. **Cross-Reference Validation**: Ensure consistency with related documents
3. **Assumption Documentation**: Make all key assumptions explicit and justified
4. **Executive Summary Quality**: Ensure 1-page summary captures most critical insights
